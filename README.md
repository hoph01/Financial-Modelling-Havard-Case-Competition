# GCCH 2026 – Thales Strategic M&A Case Analysis

## Overview
This repository contains our submission for the **Global Case Competition at Harvard 2026 (GCCH 2026)**. Our project evaluates a major strategic question in the European defense sector: whether **Thales** should pursue a merger with **Rheinmetall** or take a different path to strengthen its long-term competitive position.

Through industry research, company analysis, valuation, and strategic assessment, our team developed a recommendation focused on financial feasibility, strategic fit, and long-term value creation.

## Case Focus
The case centers on the defense and aerospace industry, with particular attention to:

- European defense market growth
- Rising geopolitical and NATO-driven defense spending
- Strategic positioning in land systems, air defense, and C4ISR
- M&A decision-making in a highly regulated industry

## What This Project Includes
This project covers the following areas:

- **Industry Analysis**  
  Overview of the European defense landscape and major market trends

- **Company Analysis**  
  Assessment of Thales and Rheinmetall, including positioning and strategic fit

- **Financial Analysis**  
  Comparable company analysis, discounted cash flow valuation, and synergy assessment

- **Acquisition Feasibility**  
  Review of regulatory, political, and execution-related constraints

- **Alternative Strategic Solution**  
  Evaluation of RENK as the preferred partner for Thales

## Key Takeaways
- The European defense market is expanding rapidly, especially in land systems and defense electronics.
- Thales is highly competitive in electronics and C4ISR, but weaker in propulsion and heavy land systems.
- Rheinmetall is a strong company, but it is more of a competitor than a complementary partner.
- RENK fills a critical capability gap for Thales and creates a clearer path toward an integrated land defense offering.
- A Thales–RENK combination appears more strategically coherent and financially practical.
